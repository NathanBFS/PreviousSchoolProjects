  • Weapon Definitions

    Handgun

    A handgun is any firearm that can be held and fired with one hand; a revolver or a pistol.

    SMG

    A submachine gun (SMG) is an automatic carbine, designed to fire pistol cartridges.

    Shotgun

    A shotgun (also known as a scattergun and peppergun,[1] or historically as a fowling piece) is a firearm that is usually designed to be fired from the shoulder, which uses the energy of a fixed shell to fire a number of small spherical pellets called shot, or a solid projectile called a slug.

    Rifle

    A rifle is a firearm designed to be fired from the shoulder, with a barrel that has a helical groove or pattern of grooves ("rifling") cut into the barrel walls.

    Bow

    A bow is an archery weapon that uses elasticity to propel arrows

    Sword

    A sword is a bladed weapon of more than 50 cm (20 in) in overall length[1] used primarily for cutting or thrusting.

    Axe

    The axe, or ax, is an implement that has been used for millennia to shape, split and cut wood; to harvest timber; as a weapon; and as a ceremonial or heraldic symbol.

    Dagger

    A dagger is a fighting knife with a sharp point designed or capable of being used as a thrusting or stabbing weapon.

    Mace

    A mace is a weapon with a heavy head on a solid shaft used to bludgeon opponents.

    Explosive

    An explosive is a reactive substance that contains a great amount of potential energy that can produce an explosion if released suddenly, usually accompanied by the production of light, heat, sound, and pressure.

    Other

    Any other item that can be used with the aim of causing damage or harm to living beings, artificial structures or systems.
